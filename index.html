What Sawari Does?

Three Domains of
Intelligence

Sawari's analytical engine spans three interconnected domains — each a specialist module feeding into a unified signal framework for comprehensive animal understanding.

01
Movement Analysis

Quantifies gait patterns, stride symmetry, rhythm, and movement quality using computer vision — no wearables needed.

02
Conformation Analysis

Extracts structural alignment indicators, evaluating skeletal proportions and conformation quality from visual input.

03
Performance Monitoring

Tracks competition and training performance across racing, endurance, dressage, and jumping disciplines with longitudinal context.

Process

From Video to Insight

01
Capture

Record your horse using a standard smartphone camera. Sawari's guided capture system validates angle, distance, and lighting in real time.

02
Analyze

Multiple analytical modules simultaneously process the data — extracting movement, conformation, behavior, and other domain-specific signals.

03
Normalize

Sawari's ASI engine translates multiple analytical outputs into a unified intelligence layer, weighted by data quality and confidence.

04
Interpret

Results map to performance bands and compare against your horse's baseline history, surfacing trends and deviations over time.

Why Sawari?

Objective Analysis Beyond Subjective Observation

The trained eye is invaluable — but it tires, it disagrees from one expert to the next, and it can't recall exactly how a horse moved last month. Sawari turns everyday video into consistent, measurable data, so every assessment is repeatable, comparable over time, and grounded in evidence rather than impression.
